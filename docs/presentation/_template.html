The Current System

Where calibration breaks down today.

How we do it today

9 manual steps per calibration

  1. Work order raised in MEX
  2. Write on a piece of blank paper, or print a blank template
  3. Fill out the instrument span in the field
  4. Handwrite as-found and as-left readings on paper
  5. Manually calculate max error per test point
  6. Walk back to the desk
  7. Scan the filled-out template into email, or re-type into a blank template
  8. Download and rename the document
  9. Attach to MEX work order + save a copy to the L: drive

Recommendations

Rule-based insights, surfaced automatically.

CalCheq runs a rule-based engine across every approved calibration — criticality, due-date status, as-found/as-left results, drift projection, and stability. Every card includes a Recommended action.
Critical
  • Safety-critical instrument past its due date
  • Last calibration could not be brought back within tolerance (as-left = fail)
  • Large drift — last as-found error exceeded 5% of span
  • Drift projection shows instrument is currently over tolerance
  • 3+ consecutive as-found failures — chronic bad actor
Advisory
  • Last result was marginal (as-found > 80% of tolerance)
  • Non-critical instrument overdue
  • Projected to exceed tolerance within 30 days
Optimisation
  • Stable for 3+ cals (peak as-found < 20% of tolerance) — consider extending interval
Drift Alerts

Linear regression on every instrument's error trend.

CalCheq plots as-found error over time, calculates drift rate, and projects the date each instrument is expected to exceed tolerance. Act before it fails — not after.

  • Sparkline trend for every instrument at a glance · Projected failure date from historical drift.
  • Sorted worst-first so the riskiest assets surface to the top · Full drift chart with tolerance band and projection on any instrument detail page.
Repeat Failures

Calibration Certificate

ISO/IEC 17025:2017 Compliant. In one click.

Calibration records are not just maintenance paperwork — they are evidence that process and safety-critical instruments remain reliable, traceable and fit for service.

  • Minimum Required Information
  • Instrument identification (tag, description, location)
  • Calibration date and next due date
  • Procedure / calibration method used
  • Test equipment used & calibration status
  • Acceptance tolerance
  • As-found results
  • Adjustments / repairs completed
  • As-left results
  • Pass / fail decision
  • Technician name / sign-off
  • Reviewer approval for critical devices
  • Traceability statement
  • Comments / defects / returned to service status
