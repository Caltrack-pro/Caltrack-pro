## Slide 7 — Smart Analytics
**~2 minutes total — ~40 seconds per tab**

> Beyond just tracking calibrations — this is where the platform earns its keep.

### Tab 1: Recommendations *(default)*

> CalCheq looks at every instrument's history and surfaces recommendations automatically.
>
> So if an instrument is consistently well inside tolerance — it'll suggest extending the interval. Save labour.
>
> If one's drifting faster than the others — shorten the interval.
>
> Repeat failures — flag for replacement.
>
> It's not AI magic. It's rule-based logic applied across the whole fleet, so the stuff that would normally require someone to sit down and analyse it — gets surfaced automatically.

*[Click the "Drift Alerts" tab]*

### Tab 2: Drift Alerts

> This is the one I'm most proud of.
>
> For every instrument, CalCheq plots as-found error over time, runs a linear regression, and **projects the date it's going to exceed tolerance.**
>
> So you're not finding out at the next annual calibration that it's failed — you're finding out three months in advance that it's going to.
>
> That means preventative work instead of reactive. Fewer surprises. Fewer trips.

*[Click the "Repeat Failures" tab]*

### Tab 3: Repeat Failures

> Bad actors. Instruments that fail over and over.
>
> Today, we know who the problem instruments are because we've been chasing them for years. CalCheq surfaces them automatically — ranked, filtered, with the full history one click away.
>
> Makes replacement decisions defensible. It's not "I reckon this one's dodgy." It's "this instrument has failed four of the last five calibrations — here's the data."
